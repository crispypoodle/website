No Ordinary Sun

"No Ordinary Sun" is a poem by Hone Tuwhare, one of New Zealand's most distinguished poets.

The poem, initially published in 1964, stands as a pinnacle of Hone Tuwhare's poetic repertoire, garnering widespread acclaim for its profound message and evocative imagery. Within its verses, Tuwhare confronts the harrowing reality of nuclear testing conducted by the British government, focusing keenly on its devastating impact on the Pacific region, notably the Marquesas Islands.

About Hone Tuwhare

Hone Tuwhare (1922–2008), of Te Ati Awa descent, is celebrated as one of New Zealand's foremost poets, his works resonating globally. Through collections like "No Ordinary Sun" (1964), Tuwhare articulated the human condition with poignant clarity, exploring themes ranging from environmentalism to personal resilience. "Come Rain Hail" (1970) delves into the resilience of the human spirit amidst adversity, while "Shape-Shifter" (1997) weaves together elements of myth and personal introspection.
